Cybercity Drive: A New Visual Chapter

The hero background had been the same abstract vaporwave texture since Phase 20 — a dark, moody piece chosen for atmosphere over narrative. It worked as a foundation. But it always felt like an abstract mood board rather than a statement of place.

The new image is more deliberately symbolic. A hovering car blazes down a road flanked by sunflowers, driving toward a neon-lit cybercity skyline beneath a giant retro sun. Laser grids cross the sky. Mountains anchor the horizon. It is, unmistakably, the Sunflower Cybercity made literal — the same aesthetic that lives in the 3D Hero scene, now rendered as a flat narrative image.

The Fade — A Transition Documented Here

At launch, the swap was implemented as a live crossfade on every page load: the old vaporwave texture visible for a half-second beat before dissolving out over three seconds as the new image faded in. Both animations ran simultaneously — old receding, new arriving — crossing at 50% midway. It acknowledged the history of where this visual identity started.

That transition has since been retired from the live hero. The two images are preserved here as the visual record of the shift. The old background doesn't get deleted — it recedes into the archive.

What the Image Represents

The car is the spaceship's surface-level counterpart — the same forward momentum, but grounded. The sunflowers are still here. The city is still lit. The laser grid overhead is the same wireframe logic that runs the 3D scene. This image collapses the entire Sunflower Cybercity into a single frame: movement, nature, technology, and the open road forward.

Every visual decision on this site is an iteration. This one just happens to be the clearest one yet.
