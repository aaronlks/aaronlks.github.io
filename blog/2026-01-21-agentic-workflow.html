Embracing the Agentic Workflow

Status: Living Document | Last updated: 2026-01-31

Today marks a milestone in how I approach development. Following up on my last LinkedIn post about the need to "learn to use AI properly", I decided to put that philosophy to the test on a personal project. What started as a rebranding exercise evolved into a creative playground for exploring how to actually bridge human intent with AI execution. After stumbling upon Google Antigravity in the "public interwebs" I realized I had the perfect opportunity to see if I could get an AI to act as a true agent of my intent.

This portfolio isn't just a resume site; it's a testament to a symbiotic co-authoring within what I call the Sunflower Cybercity. Instead of hand-coding every line as I usually would, I focused on Context Engineering: distilling a semantic mental framework from over 10 years of experience into a framework that could steer the AI’s logic and architecture effectively. It was a great exercise in moving beyond just "checking outputs" and seeing if I could apply the #KeepThinking and #SenseMaking philosophies to a modern development workflow. This site remains a "living document" of that ongoing journey.

What is it?

It's a process where I define the intent and context, the "why" and "what", and let an AI agent handle the execution, the "how". This isn't just about code generation; it's about architectural reasoning, design implementation, and rapid iteration.

The Process

  1. Context Engineering: I provided the agent with a semantic mental framework distilled from my 10+ years of experience.
  2. Iterative Refinement: We moved from broad strokes (layout, color palette) to fine details (shimmer effects, routing).
  3. Symbiosis: I focused on the creative direction and content strategy, while the agent handled the React components, Tailwind styling, and routing logic.

The Ecosystem

To maintain this workflow, we established clear roles and a "legislative" framework for the project.

Core Agents

  • Antigravity Agent (The Architect): Powered by Gemini 3 Pro, responsible for orchestration, build processes, and maintaining context awareness.
  • Context Engineer (The User): Responsible for the high-level vision, aesthetic direction, and "steering" the agent via natural language.

The Rules Mechanism

We implemented a formal Rules Mechanism (docs/RULES.md) to ensure the project remains a "living" entity. This document mandates that:

  • Documentation is Code: Key documentation files (agents.md and prompts.md) are treated as source of truth.
  • Synchronization: The architectural walkthrough and prompt history must be updated with every significant change.
  • Context Loading: The agent must read these rules and the latest blog posts at the start of every session to stay aligned with the project's meta-context.

This is the future of development, and I'm excited to explore it further.

Project Timeline & Evolution

This project has evolved rapidly through distinct eras, each building upon the last to refine the vision.

1. Foundation (Phases 1-5)

We established the core identity, high-fidelity aesthetics, glassmorphism, and the "Sunflower City" art direction. The focus was on component structure (Hero, About, Projects) and ensuring a responsive, accessible layout.

2. Refinement (Phases 6-15)

Visuals were polished with "Smart Glass" navigation, scroll progress indicators, and enhanced typography. We prepared for scalability by integrating Decap CMS and refining the content strategy to focus on high-impact professional work.

3. Optimization (Phases 16-20)

We introduced feature flags to manage complexity and improved performance by optimizing assets. The "Hybrid 3D Scene" was implemented as a hidden easter egg (toggle with 'X'), balancing technical innovation with a clean initial user experience.

4. Documentation as Code (Phase 21-22)

We shifted to a "Living Documentation" model. Static docs were transformed into this active blog, rules were codified to ensure consistency, and the agentic workflow itself became a central feature of the portfolio's narrative.

5. Build, Deployment & Content Optimization (Phase 23)

We optimized the build system to support offline CMS access by migrating cleanup logic from local scripts to the GitHub Actions workflow. This ensures a clean production build while maintaining local flexibility.

6. Feature Expansion (Phases 24-25)

We integrated a full Resume Generation backend (JSON Resume -> PDF) and refined the site's navigation structure by consolidating the Experiments section onto the homepage. This demonstrates the workflow's ability to handle full-stack capabilities (logic/scripts) alongside frontend polish.

7. Resume Integration (Phase 26)

We replaced the static PDF generation with a dynamic /resume route. This page renders directly from a resume.json source of truth, ensuring that the resume is always up-to-date with the latest data and styled consistently with the rest of the portfolio. A professional layout and print optimization were added to bridge the gap between digital and traditional formats.

8. Data Architecture & CMS (Phase 27)

We unified the data flow by decoupling content from components. projects and skills were abstracted into a central resume.json located in public/cms, serving as the Single Source of Truth for the entire application. This enables offline CMS editing and ensures perfect synchronization between the portfolio showcases (Projects/Skills sections) and the formal Resume.

9. Temporal Context & Philosophy (Phase 28-29)

We enhanced the professional narrative by adding temporal context (start/end dates) to key projects, improving scannability for recruiters and peers. Finally, we integrated a deeper personal ethos into the Hero section. By weaving the "Keep Thinking" philosophy and the concept of co-authoring with AI into the introduction, the portfolio now better reflects the symbiotic relationship between human intent and machine execution that defines my current workflow.

10. Creative Musings: On the Sunflower Cybercity (Phase 30)

The workflow evolved into a purely creative exercise—deconstructing the visual landscape of the Hero section. In "On the Sunflower Cybercity," we explored the symbolic meaning behind the Retrosun, the City, and the Sunflower fields, codifying the "Sunflower Cybercity" aesthetic. This marked our transition from technical implementation to philosophical synthesis, co-authored in a true partnership between conscious intent and AI execution.

11. 3D Evolution & Philosophical Refinement (Phases 31-33)

We evolved the "Vaporware City" experiment into a higher-fidelity "Sunflower Cybercity" 3D scene, introducing the "Purple Peaks" (Mountains) as a grounding element. This period focused on deepening the conceptual foundations of the project, ensuring that every element, from the 3D materials to the core narrative, contributes to a cohesive mental framework distilled from a decade of experience. This crystallized into a refined discovery narrative, documenting the transition from exhaustive manual hand-coding to the current agentic model that enables the development of end-to-end working solutions.

12. Narrative & Typographic Optimization (Phase 34)

We finalized the project's launch state by enforcing strict typographic standards (eliminating narrative em-dashes for cleaner flow) and refining the core narrative text. The Hero section was reorganized to group the "#KeepThinking" and "#SenseMaking" philosophies into a unified "Agentic Logic" block, creating a stronger conceptual link between the "Sunflower Cybercity" aesthetic and the practical workflow. "Google Antigravity AI Workflows" was codified as the official platform branding, completing the transition from experimental prototype to a polished, professional portfolio.

13. Deep Context Engineering (Phase 35)

We elevated the concept of "Context Engineering" from a workflow methodology to a core professional insight. The Hero quote was moved to a dedicated section and refined to articulate that web development experience is fundamentally about "text and data mining analysis." This shift emphasizes that deep domain knowledge is the prerequisite for effectively steering AI agents, moving beyond simple prompt engineering to true architectural direction. We also standardized typography across the site, establishing a consistent visual hierarchy that mirrors the structured logic of the underlying codebase.

14. Mobile Optimization (Phase 36)

We addressed a critical mobile usability issue in the Hero section where the primary call-to-action buttons were overflowing the viewport on narrow screens. By implementing a wrapping layout (flex-wrap), we ensured that the "View Projects", "Contact Me", and "Resume" buttons stack gracefully on mobile devices while maintaining their layout on larger screens. This reinforces our commitment to a polished, accessible user experience across all devices.
